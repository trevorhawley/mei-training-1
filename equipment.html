What we shoot with

Equipment

The cameras, lenses, and small accessories we use to photograph cats who would rather not be photographed.

Some links on this page are affiliate links. If you buy through them, we get a small commission at no extra cost to you. It helps keep the site ad-free. We only list gear we actually use.

Cameras

Primary body

Fujifilm X-T5

A 40-megapixel APS-C body that's small enough not to intimidate the cat. The retro dials mean we're not menu-diving while a sleeping cat is about to wake up.

  • Why it works for cats: quiet shutter, flip-out screen for low-angle shots
  • Used for: 80% of the photos on this site
Backup / travel

Ricoh GR IIIx

A pocket compact with an APS-C sensor. Fits in a jeans pocket, turns on in under a second, and is nearly silent. The camera we grab when the cat is already asleep and we don't want to blow it.

  • Why it works for cats: invisible to the subject, always with you
  • Used for: the candid "I can't believe this is happening" shots

Lenses

Workhorse

XF 23mm f/2

A 35mm-equivalent prime. Small, weather-sealed, sharp. Wide enough for context (the box, the shelf, the room), tight enough that the cat is still the subject.

Close-up

XF 80mm macro

For the paw-tucked-under-the-chin shots. Lets us fill the frame with a sleeping face without getting close enough to wake the cat.

Distance

XF 50-140mm f/2.8

For cats who notice the camera. Lets us shoot from across the room and still get a tight frame. Heavier than we'd like; worth it.

Small things that matter

A soft shutter release

Reduces the tiny "clack" of the shutter. Doesn't matter for most cats. Matters for the skittish ones.

A small continuous light

A tiny LED panel, never pointed at the cat. Just enough to bounce off the ceiling so we don't have to use a flash. Flash wakes cats. Always.

Patience

Not technically gear. But the single most important thing in the bag. We wait. The cat does the rest.

Cat things we actually recommend

Not cameras. Things for the cats themselves — the stuff that's earned a permanent spot in our homes.

Enrichment

A cardboard box. Literally any.

We're not joking. The most-used cat item in our house is a delivery box that's been on the floor for three weeks. Free. Universally loved. No affiliate link needed.

Hydration

A pet water fountain

Cats drink more from moving water. A quiet fountain (we like stainless steel, easy to clean) made a visible difference in our cats' water intake.

Play

A wand toy with a feather end

The single best dollar-to-enrichment ratio in cat ownership. Replace the feather when it gets destroyed (it will). The wand lasts forever.

Comfort

A heated cat bed

A low-wattage heated bed is the one purchase that consistently beats the cardboard box. Especially in winter. Especially for older cats.
